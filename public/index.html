About

Indian Premiere League(IPL) is a Twenty20 cricket league in India contested every year and is the most attended cricket league in the world. Bidding in IPL auction is considered as one of the most important as well as exciting opportunity for the bidders all across the places. IPL auction is a fun event that thrills the cricket lover that everyone has inside them! E- Cell VSSUT Burla has been doing IPL auction on a very big scale since 4 years under the technomanagement fest of university. With the shift of mostly everything online, we have come across the fact that it will be as much fun virtually as it has been in the university. It lets the participants to put themselves in the shoes of a bidder to build the dream team they always thought of and the one with best bargaining and bidding skills wins.

Rules

Rule 1:

Only the 8 IPL participating franchises will have the opportunity to bid in the auction namely Chennai Super Kings, Mumbai Indians, Kolkata Knight Riders, Sunrisers Hydrabad, Kings XI Punjab, Delhi Capitals, Rajasthan Royals, Royal Challengers Bangalore.

Rule 2:

  • The Auction committee will be brought in order by the acting Board members.
  • In case of any issues, Chairperson may rule over the issue and decide over the matter.

Rule 3:

  • The squad strength must be between 18 and 25 players, with a maximum of 8 overseas players and a minimum of 4 overseas players.
  • Each team will have 4 overseas players and 7 Indian (capped or uncapped) players in their playing 11.
  • Playing 11 should include:
    1. Batsmen-4
    2. Wicket keeper-1
    3. All rounders-2
    4. Bowlers-4
  • Participants have to follow the above mentioned criteria’s to avoid negative marking, which may result into disqualifications.

Rule 4:

Each team will have a salary cap of Rupees 100 crores .participants have to strictly adhere to this salary cap to avoid negative marking which may result into disqualification.

Rule 5:

  • The bidding starts from the base price of the player.
  • At a time only two teams can be involved in the bidding process.
  • The increase of bid order is as follows
    1. Below 100 Lakhs = 5 Lakhs bid increase
    2. In between 100L to 200L = 10L bid increase
    3. In between 200L to 500L = 20L bid increase
    4. Above 500L = 50 L bid increase

Rule 6:

Once the gavel is down, the players stands sold to the highest bidder unless and until there is are any discrepancies.

Rule 7:

  • The auction will be conducted in sets each set consisting of 10-12 players of same role type(ex-batsman).
  • The sets will be divided into 4 categories namely :
    1. Batsman
    2. Wicketkeepers
    3. All-rounders
    4. Bowlers

Rule 8:

  • Upon request from the teams, unsold players can be brought back into the auction in the last session of the auction.
  • Each team will be asked to mention a specified number of player’s name that they want to bring back in the auction.

Rule 9:

At the end of the auction, each franchise will submit a team of playing 11, consisting of 7 Indian players, 4 overseas players including. The franchise also has to appoint a captain and a vice captain for the same. This team should be formed based on the criteria mentioned in Rule 3 sub clause 3.

Rule 10:

  • At the end all the ratings of the players in the squad will be summed.
  • For each 1 crores remaining in the purse a rating of 100 will be added to the total ratings.
  • The total ratings of the team will be calculated as:
    Total= Sum of the ratings of the players bought in the auction + (amount remaining in the purse)*100.
